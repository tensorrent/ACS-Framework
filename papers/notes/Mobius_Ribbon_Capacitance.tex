\begin{abstract}
The companion framed-unknot electron note~\cite{mobius2026} obtains
$\alpha^{-1}\approx 137.036$ from an annulus / thin-ring capacitance
and a self-stress match $(e/2)^2/(2C)=m_e c^2$.  That note
(\S4.1) asks for a conformal-map and boundary-integral revision of
$C$.  This sequel implements three as-reported models at fixed
spin-radius input $R=\hbar/(2m_e c)$: (i)~the baseline annulus
formula; (ii)~a double-cover strip$\to$annulus conformal-modulus
image; (iii)~constant-panel single-layer BIE collocation on a
discretized M\"obius ribbon about the $(2,1)$ centerline.  Aspect
ratios are scanned; capacitances and $\alpha^{-1}$ are written to
machine-readable results.  All outputs are model claims under stated
inputs.  Comparison uses the CODATA-style reference
$\alpha^{-1}\approx 137.035999084$; no uniqueness or derivation claim
for $\alpha$ is made.
\end{abstract}

\section{Scope and claim discipline}
\label{sec:scope}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.3em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{In scope:} numerical $C$ and $\alpha^{-1}$ under three
        explicit capacitance models; aspect-ratio sensitivity; comparison
        to the CODATA-style reference value of $\alpha^{-1}$.
  \item \textbf{Out of scope:} a proof that any model yields the measured
        fine-structure constant; QED radiative corrections; a first-principles
        prediction of $a/R$ from the Standard Model.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Reference.}
Unless otherwise noted, numerical comparisons use
\begin{equation}
\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{ref}} \approx 137.035999084
\end{equation}
(CODATA-style).  Runtime constants follow \texttt{scipy.constants}
(float64), whose reciprocal fine-structure value is
$\alpha^{-1}\approx 137.035999178$ in the recorded run.

\section{Self-stress match (unchanged)}
\label{sec:match}

With charge split $e/2$ per double-cover sheet and rest-energy matching,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:Ecap}
\frac{(e/2)^2}{2C} = m_e c^2.
\end{equation}
Combined with $\alpha=e^2/(4\pi\epsilon_0\hbar c)$ and
$R=\hbar/(2m_e c)$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:alphaC}
\alpha^{-1} = \frac{\pi\epsilon_0 R}{C}.
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:alphaC} converts any positive finite $C$ into a model
$\alpha^{-1}$.

\section{Three capacitance models}
\label{sec:models}

\subsection{Annulus / thin-ring baseline}
\label{sec:annulus}

As in~\cite{mobius2026},
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:Cann}
C_{\mathrm{ann}}
=
\frac{2\pi\epsilon_0 R}{\ln(8R/a)+1}.
\end{equation}
Under~\eqref{eq:alphaC} this collapses to the closed form
\begin{equation}
\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{ann}} = \tfrac12\bigl(\ln(8R/a)+1\bigr).
\end{equation}
Matching $\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{ref}}$ forces an extreme aspect ratio
$a/R = 8\exp(1-2\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{ref}})\sim 2\times 10^{-118}$.
That match is a tuning of the cutoff, not a geometric prediction of $a$.

\subsection{Conformal double-cover strip $\to$ annulus}
\label{sec:conformal}

Take a rectangular strip of length $L=\mathrm{cover}\cdot 2\pi R$
(default $\mathrm{cover}=2$) and width $W=2a$.  The exponential map
sends the strip to an annulus with modulus
\begin{equation}
\rho = e^{2\pi W/L} = \exp\!\bigl(2a/(\mathrm{cover}\, R)\bigr).
\end{equation}
Geometric-mean radius $R_m = L/(2\pi)=\mathrm{cover}\, R$ and effective
half-width
\begin{equation}
a_{\mathrm{eff}} = R_m\,\sinh\!\bigl(a/(\mathrm{cover}\, R)\bigr)
\end{equation}
are inserted into the same logarithmic scheme as~\eqref{eq:Cann}:
\begin{equation}
C_{\mathrm{conf}}
=
\frac{2\pi\epsilon_0 R_m}{\ln(8 R_m/a_{\mathrm{eff}})+1}.
\end{equation}
This is a conformal-modulus revision of the cutoff geometry, still
within the thin-ring capacitance scheme.

\subsection{BIE / collocation on a M\"obius ribbon}
\label{sec:bie}

Discretize the immersed ribbon
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{X}(u,v)=\mathbf{r}(u)+v\,\mathbf{n}(u),
\qquad u\in[0,2\pi),\quad v\in[-a,a],
\end{equation}
about the $(2,1)$ centerline of~\cite{mobius2026}, with $\mathbf{n}$ a
torus-based M\"obius half-twist frame (stable against Frenet flips).
Constant-panel collocation of the single-layer potential at unit
potential yields $C_{\mathrm{BIE}}=Q/V$.  Lengths are nondimensionalized
by $R$; the SI capacitance is $C=C_{\hat{}}\,\epsilon_0 R$.
Mesh parameters in the recorded run: $n_u=40$, $n_v=5$ on the scan
(demo point $n_u=48$, $n_v=6$).  BIE is reported only on a moderate
aspect window where the half-width is panel-resolved
($5\times 10^{-3}\le a/R\le 0.25$).

\subsection{Ordering on the scanned grid}

On the recorded $a/R$ grid (including the BIE window),
\begin{equation}
\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{ann}}
\;>\;
\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{conf}}
\;>\;
\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{BIE}}
\end{equation}
at shared aspect ratio: the conformal double-cover image increases $C$
relative to the single-radius annulus scheme, and the immersed ribbon
BIE increases $C$ further.  None of the moderate-aspect samples approach
$\alpha^{-1}_{\mathrm{ref}}$; the annulus/conformal approach to the
reference value requires the extreme cutoffs above.

\begin{remark}[Reading of~\cite{mobius2026}'s $137.036$]
The companion value $\alpha^{-1}\approx 137.036$ is recovered by the
annulus closed form at a tuned $a/R$.  Under the conformal and BIE
revisions implemented here, the same moderate geometric aspect does
\emph{not} reproduce that figure.  This is the geometric-fidelity
caveat of~\cite{mobius2026}, \S4.1, made quantitative.
\end{remark}
