\begin{abstract}
The companion nuclear-decay note~\cite{NuclearDecay2026} extracts
$\log_{10} P_\alpha$ from Geiger--Nuttall intercepts and lifetime ratios
on a 14-isotope set ($^{212}\mathrm{Po}$--$^{244}\mathrm{Cm}$), without
predicting preformation from interior structure.  This sequel evaluates a
\emph{geometric/structural} standing-wave overlap proxy on the throat
interval $[0,R]$: the $\ell=0$ confined flag reduced radial mode
$u_{\mathrm{in}}(r)\propto r\,j_0(\pi r/R)$ against an alpha-cluster
Gaussian/HO trial $u_\alpha(r)\propto r\,\ee^{-r^2/(2a_\alpha^2)}$ with
$a_\alpha=r_0\cdot 4^{1/3}$ and input $r_0\approx 1.2\,\mathrm{fm}$.
Normalized overlap $P_{\mathrm{model}}$ is compared to table-extracted
$\log_{10} P_\alpha$.  On the stated set we report
$\langle\log_{10} P_{\mathrm{model}}\rangle=-0.3905$, Pearson correlation
$r=+0.8882$ versus extracted values, and RMS residual $1.7705$ at $S=1$.
A single global spectroscopic factor $S\approx 2.70\times 10^{-2}$
reduces RMS residual to $0.8220$ without changing the correlation
(affine shift).  Absolute scale may require such a global $S$; this is
not a many-body uniqueness claim.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation and scope}

In~\cite{NuclearDecay2026}, the Gamow factor $W$ and transfer-matrix
transmission fix the \emph{slope} of alpha-decay systematics on the stated
isotope set, while $P_\alpha$ remains an intercept-extracted nuclear-structure
amplitude.  The natural structural question is whether a confined
standing-wave mode on $[0,R]$ overlaps an alpha-like radial packet enough
to track the extracted $\log_{10} P_\alpha$ trend.

\begin{remark}[RC1 scope]
$P_{\mathrm{model}}$ is a geometric/structural preformation \emph{proxy}.
It is not a first-principles shell-model or R-matrix spectroscopic factor,
and absolute calibration may need one global factor $S$.  Half-lives enter
only through the extracted comparison column already published
in~\cite{NuclearDecay2026}; they are not fitted into $P_{\mathrm{model}}$.
\end{remark}

\section{Radial modes}

\subsection{Confined flag mode ($\ell=0$)}
Inside the throat the reduced radial amplitude for the first Dirichlet zero
of $j_0$ is
\begin{equation}
u_{\mathrm{in}}(r) = r\, j_0(k r),\qquad k=\frac{\pi}{R},
\end{equation}
so $u_{\mathrm{in}}(R)=0$.  Equivalently $u_{\mathrm{in}}(r)=\sin(kr)/k$ on
$(0,R]$.

\subsection{Alpha-cluster trial}
We take a Gaussian / harmonic-oscillator $0s$ packet truncated to the
integration window $[0,R]$:
\begin{equation}
u_\alpha(r) = r\,\ee^{-r^2/(2 a_\alpha^2)},\qquad
a_\alpha = r_0\, 4^{1/3},
\end{equation}
with input $r_0=1.2\,\mathrm{fm}$, hence $a_\alpha\approx 1.905\,\mathrm{fm}$.

\subsection{Overlap definition}
\begin{equation}
P_{\mathrm{model}}
=
\frac{\bigl|\displaystyle\int_0^R u_{\mathrm{in}}(r)\,u_\alpha(r)\,\dd r\bigr|^2}
{\displaystyle
\Bigl(\int_0^R u_{\mathrm{in}}^2\,\dd r\Bigr)
\Bigl(\int_0^R u_\alpha^2\,\dd r\Bigr)}.
\end{equation}
Optional global scale: $P_S = S\, P_{\mathrm{model}}$, with $S$ fitted so that
$\langle\log_{10} P_S\rangle=\langle\log_{10} P_\alpha^{\mathrm{ext}}\rangle$.

\section{Numerical protocol}

Integrals use Simpson quadrature on a uniform grid of $N=4096$ points on
$[0,R]$.  Throat radii $R$ and extracted $\log_{10} P_\alpha$ are taken from
the 14-row table in~\cite{NuclearDecay2026}.  Implementation path (repo):
\texttt{code/palpha\_overlap/palpha\_overlap.py}; results JSON:
\texttt{docs/palpha\_overlap\_results.json}.

\subsection{Summary statistics}
On the stated 14-isotope set ($r_0=1.2\,\mathrm{fm}$, $N=4096$):
\begin{align*}
\langle\log_{10} P_{\mathrm{model}}\rangle &= -0.3905
\quad(\sigma=0.0142),\\
\langle\log_{10} P_\alpha^{\mathrm{ext}}\rangle &= -1.9586
\quad(\sigma=0.8656),\\
r\bigl(\log_{10} P_{\mathrm{model}},\,\log_{10} P_\alpha^{\mathrm{ext}}\bigr)
&= +0.8882,\\
\mathrm{RMS}_{S=1} &= 1.7705,\qquad
S_{\star}=2.703\times 10^{-2},\qquad
\mathrm{RMS}_{S_\star}=0.8220.
\end{align*}
Pearson $r$ is unchanged by the global $S$ (pure vertical shift in
$\log_{10}$).  $S$ improves the absolute-scale RMS match by
$\Delta\mathrm{RMS}=+0.9485$.

\begin{remark}[Interpretation]
Because $a_\alpha$ is fixed and $R$ varies only weakly across the set,
$\log_{10} P_{\mathrm{model}}$ spans a narrow band ($\sigma\approx 0.014$)
while extracted $\log_{10} P_\alpha$ spans $\sigma\approx 0.87$.  The
proxy therefore captures a mild $R$-driven geometric trend correlated with
extracted preformation ($r\approx 0.89$), but leaves isotope-specific
structure in the residual.  A single global $S$ absorbs mean offset; it
does not remove residual structure.
\end{remark}

\section{Conclusion}
We have computed the standing-wave overlap sequel requested
in~\cite{NuclearDecay2026}: a confined $\ell=0$ flag mode against a
Gaussian alpha trial on $[0,R]$.  On the stated set the proxy correlates
with extracted $\log_{10} P_\alpha$ at $r=+0.8882$; absolute scale is
improved by one global $S\approx 0.027$.  This documents a geometric
preformation trend, not a unique many-body prediction.

\begin{remark}[Next step]
The flat $[0,R]$ measure and Gaussian trial are a geometric proxy.
A sequel replaces them with the AdS-like throat weight $w(r)=R/r$ from
the companion nuclear metric and a Woods--Saxon (optional Coulomb-tail)

retained for comparison.
\end{remark}
