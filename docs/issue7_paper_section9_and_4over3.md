## Abstract

This paper documents two diagnostic programs: (1) a toy test of the dependency direction `spectral gap -> clustering -> cone sharpness proxy`, and (2) a mechanism test for the numeric coincidence between an ACS-side value `4/3` and a density-side exponent form `1 + 1/d` at `d=3`. The tests are finite-model and kill-oriented by design. They are reported with explicit reproducibility artifacts (stdout logs, JSON outputs, SHA-256 anchors, and an end-to-end verification report).

For the Section 9 program, the tested model family does not produce robust support for the full monotone chain criterion across the tested ranges. For the 4/3 program, the equality fails robustness checks under normalization scaling and appears as an identity family in the scanned integer lattice. An independent verification pipeline reruns all scripts and returns `PASS_WITH_CAUTION`.

## 1. Scope and claim discipline

This document reports finite tested outcomes only. It does not claim a universal no-go theorem or a general impossibility statement. All findings are bounded by the tested model families, parameter windows, and numerical backend.

## 2. Research questions

### RQ-1 (Section 9 toy chain)

In finite lattice models, does larger spectral gap co-vary with both:

- shorter static clustering length, and
- lower outside-cone commutator leakage?

### RQ-2 (4/3 mechanism)

Is the observed equality at `4/3` generated by a robust mechanism, or does it collapse under normalization changes and bookkeeping-identity checks?

### Models

- TFIM open chain: `H = -J sum Z_i Z_{i+1} - h sum X_i`
- XXZ+h open chain: `H = Jxy sum (X_iX_{i+1}+Y_iY_{i+1}) + Jz sum Z_iZ_{i+1} - h sum X_i`
- Long-range Ising: `H = -sum_{i<j} J/|i-j|^alpha Z_i Z_j - h sum X_i`, `alpha in {3, 2}`
- Exact classical Ising + local majority influence (integer energies, `Fraction` weights)

### Observables

- Gap: `Delta = E1 - E0`
- Clustering proxy: exponential-fit length `xi` from connected `Z0-Zr` correlators
- Cone-leakage proxies from `||[Z0(t), Zr]||`:
  - outside-cone max
  - outside-cone mean (cross-model extension)

### Decision rule

Support requires negative co-variation for all required legs:

- `corr(gap, xi) < -0.2`
- `corr(gap, leak_proxy) < -0.2`

When both max and mean leak proxies are present, both must pass.

## 7. Conclusion

Within the reported finite tests, the Section 9 dependency chain is not supported as a robust monotone relation across the tested model families and thresholds. The long-range Ising family shows the highest partial-support fraction (`5/12`), concentrated at the smallest leakage threshold, which indicates the diagnostic is sensitive to threshold choice in regimes where strict cones weaken — a threshold-dependence effect, not evidence of a stable universal trend. The exact float-free analogue also fails the joint chain criterion (`cluster` cov sign correct; outside-cone affect identically zero under local majority). The 4/3 equality does not satisfy the tested mechanism robustness criteria and is consistent with an identity-family explanation on the scanned lattice. The package is reproducible in-repo with anchored artifacts; end-to-end verification retains caution only for the remaining float NumPy Section 9 scripts (see footnote).
