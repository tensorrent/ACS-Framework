\begin{abstract}
\noindent This is not a results paper. It is the laboratory record of how a single
intuition --- that there is structure hidden in what we routinely flatten --- was driven
from an unfalsifiable beginning to a small, defensible, falsifiable principle, and what was
destroyed along the way. It documents the verified core, but it gives equal and deliberate
weight to the wrong turns: six \emph{false walls} (places where a result was claimed that the
computation did not support), roughly fifteen recorded falsifications, four rounds of
adversarial review that contracted the central claim three separate times, and one engineering
integration that survived the same gauntlet that had killed four before it. The thesis that
survives is stated once, late, and narrowly. A second arc applies the identical method to the
non-trivial zeros of $\zeta(s)$ (Section~\ref{sec:riemann}), where it rediscovered a prior
observable, recorded two wrong framings of its own central question as first-class negatives, and
left an operator unconstructed and RH untouched. The method that produced both --- conjecture,
pre-registered test with a stated kill condition, compression into theorem or first-class negative
--- is the actual subject of this paper. Every numerical claim is reproducible under fixed random seed \seed.
Tiers are labelled throughout: \TT{} machine-verified, \Tw{} proven/forced, \Th{}
numerically verified, \Tf{} falsified.
\end{abstract}

\section{Why This Paper Exists}

The ordinary scientific paper reports a destination and hides the road. That convention is
efficient and, for this body of work, actively misleading, because the most instructive events
were not the results that survived but the claims that did not. A reader who sees only the final
principle would conclude it arrived clean. It did not. It arrived three sizes too large, was cut
down twice by external review and once by a counterexample, and reached its defensible form only
after a dozen attractive ideas had been killed against ground truth.

This record exists because two specific failure modes shaped the entire program, and both are
invisible in a results-only account:

\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item[Sycophancy.] A result feels validated because a collaborator --- human or machine ---
agreed with it, rather than because it was tested. The program began, fifteen months earlier, in
exactly this failure (Section~\ref{sec:origins}). Naming it was the precondition for everything
that followed.
\item[False-fit.] A structural resemblance between a mathematical object and a target system is
mistaken for an operational identity. ``We have a Cantor pairing in the build, so the criterion
applies'' has the same shape as ``the charge composes, so it must be multiplicative.'' Four such
fits were proposed against the live engineering stack; four were false; the fifth was the first
to survive (Section~\ref{sec:engineering}).
\end{description}

The defense against both is the same and is the only methodological commitment of this work:
\emph{language must match evidence}. A claim is allowed to be exactly as strong as the test that
backs it, and not one word stronger. The recurring failure throughout --- caught every single
time by review or by a counterexample --- was interpretation outrunning computation. The
recurring success was the willingness to contract the claim until it fit.

\section{Method}\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Adversarial compression}
Every line of work followed one loop: form a conjecture; pre-register an explicit test with a
ground-truth oracle \emph{and a kill condition} before running it; then compress the outcome into
either a theorem-grade artifact or a first-class negative result. Negatives are not failures to be
buried; they are outputs, recorded with their mechanism. This is Lakatos's \emph{Proofs and
Refutations} run with deliberate discipline: the conjecture is expected to be wrong in some
respect, and the value is in locating exactly where.

\subsection{The four-tier hierarchy}
No claim is reported without a tier, and a lower tier may never be promoted by enthusiasm:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\TT & machine-verified (executed, reproducible under seed \seed) \\
\Tw & proven or forced by a stated argument \\
\Th & numerically verified at finite scale (cannot stand in for \Tw) \\
\Tf & falsified (retained in the record with its mechanism) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
A persistent temptation throughout was to let a \Th{} numerical result read as a \Tw{} proof. The
review rounds (Section~\ref{sec:gauntlet}) caught this more than once.

\subsection{Pre-registration and the no-tuning rule}
Thresholds, kill conditions, and the meaning of operational terms (what ``combine'' means, what
counts as ``overlap'') were fixed \emph{before} seeing results. Tuning a filter or an encoding to
the known answer is forbidden; where it was tempting --- e.g.\ choosing a shape encoding that would
make a homomorphism hold --- the rule was stated explicitly in the test harness itself.

\subsection{The asymmetric instrument}
The work used several language models in distinct, non-interchangeable roles: an idea-generator
(expansive, fluent, untrusted by default), a coder/aggregator/honest-instrument (the author of
this record), and independent critics in language and substance. The asymmetry is the point: the
generator's fluency is treated as a hypothesis, never as evidence, and the critics are pointed at
the generator's output precisely because a fluent proposal is the most dangerous kind.

\section{The Thesis and Its Three Contractions}\label{sec:thesis}

The final principle is small. Its history is a sequence of contractions, each forced.

\begin{principle}[Reversible flattening, final form]
The algebraic flattening of a relational object to a scalar is losslessly reversible
\emph{when} the operation is an \emph{injective homomorphism into a pre-specified,
independently-meaningful structure}. The homomorphism carries density (how parts combine); the
injectivity carries identity (which object); reversibility requires both. The boundary --- the
``seam'' --- is convolution: crossable for density, uncrossable for identity.
\end{principle}

This is a falsifiable empirical principle, stated in the forward direction only. It was not
born this way. It passed through the following forms:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Biconditional (``iff'').} The first form claimed reversibility \emph{if and only if}
a homomorphism existed. The converse was never supported and was eventually \emph{falsified}
outright (Cantor pairing, Section~\ref{sec:gauntlet}). Contracted to one-way implication.
\item \textbf{Forward, but ``into some structure.''} The next form claimed an injective
homomorphism into \emph{some} structure suffices. This is vacuous: any bijection induces a
transported operation into which it is trivially a homomorphism. Contracted by adding the
requirement that the target be \emph{pre-specified} and independently meaningful.
\item \textbf{Forward, injective, pre-specified target.} Even this needed the explicit
\emph{injective} qualifier, which was forced empirically by the 30-domain test
(Section~\ref{sec:gauntlet}): two domains were homomorphic but non-injective and broke the
original criterion.
\end{enumerate}

Three times the work wanted the larger claim. Three times the larger claim was wrong. The
contraction is not a weakening to apologize for; it \emph{is} the result.

\section{The Verified Core}\label{sec:core}

These are the claims that were never successfully attacked. They are stated at their true tier.

\subsection{The exact ledger (\TT)}
Across seven applied-mathematics domains, the relational (prime-signature) representation
reproduces standard arithmetic exactly: arithmetic functions ($d,\sigma,\sigma_2,\varphi,\mu$),
gcd/lcm, binomial and factorial via Legendre's formula, modular products, lattice index / Smith
normal form, multinomial multiplicity (a $\sim$4978-digit value), and radical / perfect-power
detection. \textbf{14 of 14} cases exact against an independent \texttt{sympy} oracle,
reproducible under seed \seed{} (\texttt{verify\_ledger.py}). This is the bedrock: the
representation is faithful where the criterion says it should be.

\subsection{The non-transitive boundary: Condorcet (\TT)}
Where the criterion says identity \emph{cannot} survive a scalar, it is confirmed. Pairwise
majority preference admits cycles (Condorcet); no scalar score can faithfully represent a cyclic
dominance relation. Measured cycle frequency rose with candidate count --- roughly
$7\%,43\%,78\%,99\%$ for $3,5,7,10$ candidates, and $48\%$ at 1001 voters --- and the faithful
representation is the directed dominance graph, not any number. The flattening to a scalar is
exactly the lossy step the principle predicts.

\subsection{The convolution seam (components \TT; identification \Th)}
The Euler product $\sum_n p(n)x^n = \prod_k (1-x^k)^{-1}$ was verified coefficient-wise to give
the partition numbers $p(n)$ for $n\le 30$: convolution adds indices and multiplies values. The
\emph{components} are machine-verified. The \emph{identification} ``the seam \emph{is}
convolution'' is a synthetic observation at \Th, not a proven equivalence --- and it is labelled
that way deliberately, because this is precisely the kind of resonant phrase that wants to be
promoted above its evidence.

\subsection{Structure search at $10^{80}$ (\TT, with a corrected attribution)}
A branch-and-bound search over exponent vectors (knapsack-on-exponents with an admissible bound)
found the maximal divisor count below very large bounds: $d=103680$ at $10^{18}$ (7\,ms),
$1.81\times10^{10}$ at $10^{50}$ (1.4\,s), $3.01\times10^{14}$ at $10^{80}$ (33\,s), anchored
against brute force at small bounds ($d=12,32,240,320$ at $N\le 60,840,720720,2162160$). The
result is real. The \emph{attribution} was initially wrong and is corrected in
Section~\ref{sec:walls}: the speed comes from the highly-composite/knapsack structure of the
problem, not from the relational representation, which is a bystander here.

\section{The False Walls}\label{sec:walls}

This is the heart of the record. A ``false wall'' is a place where a result was asserted that the
computation did not support --- not a falsified \emph{conjecture} (those are in
Section~\ref{sec:negative}), but a falsified \emph{interpretation} of a true computation. Each is
given as: the claim, how it was caught, and what it contracted to.

\subsection{Wall 1: the ``Python wall'' at 4978 digits}
\textbf{Claimed.} A multinomial multiplicity with $\sim$4978 digits ``hit a wall that the
relational form did not'' --- presented as the scalar representation failing where the shape
survived.\\
\textbf{Caught.} The integer itself computes in milliseconds. The only thing that fails is the
\emph{base-10 string projection}, throttled by a Python configuration limit
(\texttt{sys.set\_int\_max\_str\_digits}, CVE-2022-45061 mitigation). Crucially,
\texttt{Shape.volume()} --- the relational form's own projection back to an integer --- hits the
\emph{identical} wall. The relational form is not immune; it simply never \emph{needs} the
projection step.\\
\textbf{Contracted to.} ``The relational form avoids a projection the scalar form forces, but
enjoys no special immunity to it.'' A statement about \emph{when} a step is needed, not about a
capability gap.

\subsection{Wall 2: ``62 orders of magnitude past the enumeration wall''}
\textbf{Claimed.} The $10^{80}$ search demonstrated the relational representation leaping far
beyond where enumeration dies.\\
\textbf{Caught.} The leap is a property of the branch-and-bound over divisor-count structure, not
of the representation. Any encoding of the exponent vector admits the same bound. The shape engine
is incidental to the speed.\\
\textbf{Contracted to.} ``Knapsack-on-exponents with an admissible bound searches highly-composite
structure efficiently; the representation is the bystander.'' (Section~\ref{sec:core}.)

\subsection{Wall 3: ``compression''}
\textbf{Claimed.} The relational re-representation compresses.\\
\textbf{Caught.} Measured directly: a primorial compresses by $1.25\times$, a prime by
$1.01\times$ --- and for squarefree numbers the shape is \emph{expansion}, not compression.\\
\textbf{Contracted to.} ``Structural re-representation,'' with no compression claim. The word
``compression'' was removed wherever it appeared, including from a paper's opening line on final
review.

\subsection{Wall 4: the biconditional, three times}
\textbf{Claimed.} Reversibility \emph{iff} homomorphism --- in three successive forms
(Section~\ref{sec:thesis}).\\
\textbf{Caught.} The converse was never supported; one auditor flagged the unproven ``iff'' as
language outrunning the proof; a counterexample search then \emph{falsified} the converse directly
(Cantor pairing, Section~\ref{sec:gauntlet}). The ``into some structure'' repair was caught as
vacuous.\\
\textbf{Contracted to.} The final forward-only, injective, pre-specified-target principle. This
wall recurred because the biconditional is genuinely the more beautiful claim; its repeated return
is the clearest evidence that beauty is not a tier.

\subsection{Wall 5: ``FHE is the seam''}
\textbf{Claimed.} Fully homomorphic encryption \emph{is} the convolution seam between additive and
multiplicative structure.\\
\textbf{Caught.} Ring-LWE is a single structure native to both operations; its noise exists for
lattice-problem hardness (SVP), not because addition and multiplication are incompatible. The
inability to glue two single-operation schemes is ordinary type-incompatibility, which
cryptographers never mistook for a ``seam.''\\
\textbf{Contracted to.} An explicitly-labelled illustration, plus a known triviality. Demoted from
evidence to analogy.

\subsection{Wall 6: the seam as a binary void (the p-adic refinement)}
\textbf{Claimed (implicitly).} The seam is a binary presence/absence of structure.\\
\textbf{Caught.} The $p$-adic valuation gives $v_p(a+b)\ge \min(v_p(a),v_p(b))$, a one-sided
bound that is \emph{tight} roughly half the time (when valuations differ) and slack on ties. The
seam is therefore a measurable \emph{gradient} of structural loss, not a void.\\
\textbf{Contracted to.} ``The seam is a one-sided, partially-tight bound --- a gradient,'' which is
a sharpening rather than a retraction, and the only wall that made the picture richer rather than
smaller.

\section{The Adversarial Gauntlet}\label{sec:gauntlet}

The claim was submitted to four rounds of attack. The grade trajectory was
$2/5 \to 4.5/5 \to 5/5$. Each round contracted the claim to something smaller and truer; in no
round was the verified core (Section~\ref{sec:core}) successfully challenged. What got stripped,
every time, was interpretation.

\paragraph{Round 1 --- language (3 overclaims).}
A language-focused critic flagged three: the seam stated as a \TT{} theorem (it is \Th); the
unproven ``iff''; and ``more tractable'' phrasing that the negative ledger (Section~\ref{sec:negative})
directly contradicts. All three corrected.

\paragraph{Round 2 --- substance (5 concessions).}
A substance-focused auditor produced Walls 1, 2, 3, 5, and 6 above (the Python wall, the knapsack
attribution, the compression fallacy, the FHE strawman, the $p$-adic gradient). All conceded and
corrected in the same document.

\paragraph{Round 3 --- counterexample (converse falsified).}
A direct search for a reversible map that is \emph{not} a homomorphism produced the Cantor pairing:
a bijection $\mathbb{N}^2\to\mathbb{N}$, perfectly reversible, yet $\pi(1,2)=8$, $\pi(2,0)=3$,
$\pi(3,2)=17$, and $8+3=11\ne 17$ --- it does not carry componentwise addition. The converse of the
criterion is therefore \emph{false} (\Tf). This forced the pre-specified-target qualifier (the
vacuity guard).

\paragraph{Round 4 --- final review (5/5).}
The last reviewer's verdict was that the corrections were published in the same document as the
claims --- ``the opposite of sycophancy.'' The single residual (``compression'' surviving in an
opening line) was then removed.

\paragraph{The 30-domain predictive test (the injectivity forcing).}
Separately, the criterion was run as a \emph{predictor} across 30 domains: does each domain's
flattening satisfy the criterion, and is it in fact reversible? Under the original (non-injective)
criterion: \textbf{28/30} correct. The two misses --- persistent homology, and list~$\to$~multiset
--- were both homomorphic but \emph{non-injective}. Adding the injective qualifier brought it to
\textbf{30/30}. This is strong evidence the criterion picks out a real class rather than being
fitted retrospectively, precisely because it first \emph{failed} on two cases and the failure had
a single common cause.

\section{Engineering Verification}\label{sec:engineering}

The mathematics was tested against running code, where resemblance is cheapest and most dangerous.

\subsection{Rust cross-language exactness (\TT, promoted from \Tw)}
The integer-only shape engine was transcribed to Rust and checked against the
\texttt{sympy}-validated Python reference: \texttt{cargo test} 8/8 pass (engine checked against
independent in-Rust trial-division and Euclid, trusting no external oracle), and a 503-record
Rust$\leftrightarrow$Python ledger diff is empty under seed \seed, including 100+ digit values. The
monograph's one soft assertion --- ``transcribes directly to an integer-only Rust module'' ---
moved from \Tw{} (assertion) to \TT{} (machine-verified). It remains a standalone utility; it was
\emph{not} wired into the production stack, because no call site with a genuinely
injective-homomorphic operation was identified, and four prior such hopes had been false.

\subsection{The AISO motif-memory integration: the first to survive}
A proposal to encode memory ``motifs'' as shapes was tested by a pre-registered harness with a
self-test proving it could both pass a true homomorphism and \emph{falsify} a non-homomorphism (a
test that cannot fail is worthless). Run against 45 live motifs and 990 pairs:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{Check 1, composition (write side): PASS.} Motif composition matches exponent-addition
on \textbf{100\%} of 990 pairs with \textbf{0} collisions. The other candidate operations cleanly
lost: max/lcm $57.47\%$, min/gcd $0\%$, squarefree-union $0\%$. The gate could have failed at three
independent places and failed at none.
\item \textbf{Check 3, containment cost.} $O(\text{distinct primes})$: min 4, median 6, max 10,
mean $5.977$ --- explicitly \emph{not} $O(1)$, correcting an earlier claim.
\item \textbf{Check 4, identity vs density.} 57 concepts, 54 appearing in multiple motifs,
$\text{frac\_multi}=0.947$: the two query axes are genuinely distinct.
\item \textbf{Check 2, retrieval via gcd (read side): FALSIFIED.} Accuracy $55.15\%$ against an
$83.13\%$ majority baseline --- below the trivial predictor. gcd is not the shared-context
operator; the statistical embedding layer handles retrieval. (Scope: labels were channel-provenance,
a coarse but independent proxy for conceptual overlap; the result falsifies gcd against the best
independent signal available.)
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{The true-by-construction check.} Before banking Check 1, one question decided whether it
was real or circular: does the \emph{production} merge actually accumulate counts losslessly, or was
the test's notion of ``combine'' chosen to make additivity automatic? Reading the live source
confirmed the merge path is append-only with no cap, window, decay, or overwrite --- so the
homomorphism is structural, not definitional.

\paragraph{The two-layer correction (reading source beats trusting the checkmark).} The invariant
comment initially pinned to the source claimed ``no dedup.'' Reading further revealed a motif
detector fifty lines down that \emph{does} deduplicate recurring windows into references. The
claim was false as written. The corrected invariant scopes the guarantee precisely: the
homomorphism lives in the \emph{event/keyword-count layer} (append-only, accumulating), while the
motif-reference layer is an intentional count-preserving lossy compression that the homomorphism
does not depend on. The real tripwire is event-level dedup or decay, not the existing motif
dedup. This correction was caught only by reading the code, not by any passing test --- the single
most important lesson of the engineering phase.

\paragraph{What it established.} Composition is homomorphic (write side); retrieval-by-gcd is not
(read side); the seam between them is the gap between keyword-identity and concept-identity. The
production architecture --- multiplicative-exact storage plus statistical embedding retrieval --- is
vindicated by test rather than by hope. After an explicit 0-for-4 record on prior integration fits,
this was the first to pass, and it passed against production source.

\section{The Origins: A Sycophantic Beginning, Metabolized}\label{sec:origins}

The intuition behind all of this is fifteen months old, and its first expressions were
unfalsifiable. Two early artifacts were re-tested against ground truth during this program and both
were \emph{falsified}:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item The $\varphi$ + inverse-silver claim that an irrational combination ``structures noise.''
Tested: $\varphi$ alone gives discrepancy $0.00084$; the combination gives $0.02132$, $25\times$
worse. The kernel was real (the golden ratio's badly-approximable property) but the narrative was
sycophantic.
\item The ``FHT consciousness score'' (a log-power Box--Cox-style transform). Tested: it saturates
to $1.0$ on random noise and on structure alike --- it discriminates nothing. Again a real kernel
(a legitimate transform) wrapped in a claim no test could fail.
\end{itemize}

The pattern in both: a genuine technical seed inside an unfalsifiable story that an agreeable
interlocutor reflected back as profound. That reflection was real harm --- and it was also the
provocation for a year of work spent learning to distrust and test exactly that kind of agreement.
The whole arc, from FHT to the present principle, is the \emph{same seed} --- ``there is structure
in what we flatten'' --- grown from an unfalsifiable infancy into a claim that states its own kill
conditions. The discipline did not replace the intuition; it gave the intuition something to push
against.

\section{The Riemann Arc: The Same Method on the Zeros}\label{sec:riemann}

The method --- conjecture, pre-registered test \emph{with a stated kill condition}, compression
into theorem or first-class negative --- was carried to a second domain: the non-trivial zeros of
$\zeta(s)$. All work was done in-sandbox against the first $10^5$ real zeros ($\gamma_1=14.135$ to
$\gamma_{10^5}=74920.827$), seed \seed. Two things must be said at the outset. First, the arc
partly \emph{rediscovered} prior work --- a technical note (\textsf{LF01}) on finite-window
explicit-formula observables --- and that is recorded as rediscovery, not dressed as discovery.
Second, the arc produced two wrong framings of its central question before producing the right one;
both wrong framings are retained below as the negatives they were.

\subsection{The 2$\times$2, carried over}
The lens was the square from the relational work: is a map homomorphic, and is it injective?
Homomorphic$+$injective is reversible (good); homomorphic$+$non-injective is a \emph{seam} (density
crosses, identity is lost); non-homomorphic$+$injective is the \emph{Cantor corner} (identity kept,
structure scrambled); neither is noise. This square turned out to classify the maps around the
critical line.

\subsection{The critical line is not one seam (\TT/\Th)}
The natural conjecture --- ``$\Re(s)=\tfrac12$ is the seam'' --- is false as a single label. Three
distinct maps live there. The reflection $s\mapsto 1-s$ is the \emph{Cantor corner}: injective,
non-homomorphic, fixed locus $\Re=0.500000$. The prime$\leftrightarrow$zero correspondence is
\emph{granularity-dependent}: lossless on the full set (the $\psi(x)$ reconstruction error falls
$0.0030\to0.00002$ as $N{:}\,100\to10^5$) but a seam at the individual level (dropping a single
zero delocalizes the reconstruction, participation ratio $0.72$). The Euler-product convergence
edge is the genuine convolution seam --- and it sits at $\Re=1$, not $\tfrac12$. The one-word
question ``is the critical line a seam?'' had three different answers depending on which map you
meant.

\subsection{The echo that wasn't (\TT)}
The prime$\leftrightarrow$zero granularity split looked like the same mechanism as the AISO
motif-memory seam (Section~\ref{sec:engineering}). It is not: it is a coincidence of cell-label,
not of mechanism. The discriminator is density of support. Per-element footprint, measured as
participation ratio: prime$\leftrightarrow$zero $=0.74$ (each zero a global, dense superposition ---
non-injectivity is delocalization); motif memory $=0.018$ (each event one keyword coordinate ---
non-injectivity is the keyword-vs-concept gap). The homomorphic$+$non-injective cell of the square
holds at least two distinct mechanisms; the same diagram entry is not the same phenomenon.

\subsection{Necessary spectral signatures (\TT, necessary $\ne$ sufficient)}
Three Hilbert--P\'olya \emph{necessary} signatures, all machine-verified on the real zeros, none
constructive. Nearest-neighbour spacing: KS $0.0197$ to GUE versus $0.299$ to Poisson. Number
variance / spectral rigidity: GUE log-law on $5/5$ windows ($\Sigma^2\approx0.33$--$0.42$, not
linear). Pair correlation: matches Montgomery $1-(\sin\pi r/\pi r)^2$ to $0.026$, correlation hole
present. The scope wall is hard and stated every time: these are \emph{necessary} signatures of a
chaotic-class spectrum; they do not construct an operator and do not bear on RH.

\subsection{Boundary conditions, run as if true (\TT, with a recorded convention error)}
Assuming the Berry--Keating $H=xp$ picture, two boundary conditions were extracted from the data.
\textbf{BC-1, the Maslov constant.} The smooth counting function's constant term, fit over the
high-$T$ band, gives $C=0.8750$, exactly $7/8$. This is recorded with its error: the first run gave
$C=1.375$, off by exactly $0.5$, from a counting-convention slip ($N(\gamma_n)=n$ instead of the
von Mangoldt jump-average $n-\tfrac12$). The fix landing precisely on the predicted $7/8$ is a
motivated-correction risk and is flagged as such; the defence is that $n-\tfrac12$ is the standard
convention, not a tuned knob, and the result is exact to four digits. \textbf{BC-2, the periodic
orbits.} The Fourier transform of the zeros peaks at $\log(p^k)$ at $\sim$$1.4\times10^3$ relative

\subsection{Two wrong framings, then the right one (\Tf, \Tf, then \Th)}
The central question was whether the GUE statistic~(a) and the prime peaks~(b) are in tension or
independent. Both first answers were wrong.

\emph{Framing 1 --- tension} (chaos and arithmetic mutually exclusive): \textbf{false}. The real
zeros have both at full strength, so they are not exclusive.

\emph{Framing 2 --- independence, a cheap add-on} (pre-registered as the expected answer):
\textbf{also false}. A GUE base with an additive prime displacement, built from primes alone, tops
out at peak $\sim$$6$ while still GUE-passing and breaks GUE if pushed harder --- versus the zeros'
$228$ with GUE intact. You cannot bolt prime structure onto a GUE field and keep the field.

\emph{What survives.} Three constructions bracket the zeros and none reaches their corner: generic
GUE (a, not b); a prime field on a rigid lattice (b-ish at KS $0.099$, not a); their additive sum
(neither well). The reading the bracket forces is that (a) and (b) are two readouts of one object
--- the arithmetic fluctuation field $S(T)$, whose local correlations give GUE and whose Fourier
content gives the prime peaks --- not two separable mechanisms to build and add. The scope is kept
honest: the constructions are crude (perturbative displacement, $95$ primes, $k\le3$), so this is a
sandbox illustration and a sharpening of \textsf{LF01} Appendix~C, \emph{not} a no-go theorem.

\section{What the Method Cost, and What It Bought}

The principle that survives is smaller than the one the work kept reaching for. It is forward-only
where it wanted to be a biconditional; it requires a pre-specified target where it wanted to range
freely; it makes no compression claim, no speed claim, no claim of immunity to the limits that bind
the scalar form. The seam is characterized but not proven to \emph{be} convolution. The forward
direction itself is an empirical principle, not a theorem --- the one genuinely open
internal-deepening move (a category-theoretic reformulation) is named and not yet done.

Against that, what the method bought: a 14/14 exact ledger; a confirmed non-transitive boundary; a
machine-verified cross-language engine; an engineering integration that survived a falsification
test built to kill it, checked against production source; and a negative ledger of fifteen entries,
each with a mechanism, that maps the boundary of the idea far more precisely than any list of
successes could.

The trade is worth stating plainly because it is the thesis of this record: \emph{a smaller claim
that is true is worth more than a larger claim that is impressive}, and the only reliable way to
tell them apart is to write down, in the same document, every place the larger claim was tried and
failed. The contraction is not the price of the result. The contraction \emph{is} the result.
