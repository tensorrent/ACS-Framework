\begin{abstract}
An earlier draft of this note called the critical line a \emph{flattening} --- a projection
of a higher object down to a line. That is the wrong word in this bundle's own vocabulary and
the wrong picture. \textit{The Reversible Flattening} fixes ``flattening'' to mean the
medium-driven compression of a \emph{relational} object to a \emph{scalar}, reversible exactly
when the compressing operation is a homomorphism; its boundary --- the \emph{seam} --- is
\emph{convolution}, ``crossable for density and uncrossable for identity.'' \textit{The Geometry
Engine} then shows the Riemann zeta function is \emph{two-sided}: a prime face (the Euler
product; multiplicative, \emph{unique} structure --- a factorization is a point) and a zero
face (the Hadamard product; additive, \emph{cardinal} structure --- the size of a cloud), the
two joined by the explicit formula and verified in both directions. The critical line is that
\textbf{seam}, not a shadow of it: a two-dimensional, two-sided object, not a flattened scalar.
We correct the framing and show our own measurements are an instance of the seam law: the
arithmetic ``face'' of the zeros ($\sim\!830\sigma$) is the prime side imaged across the seam,
the local-order face is the zero side's own cardinal structure, and the finding that arithmetic
couples to \emph{density} ($\sim\!0.99$) while individual spacings stay \emph{generic} (GUE) is
verbatim ``crossable for density, uncrossable for identity.'' We then argue the one seam has
three descriptions that the bundle verifies separately and that we conjecture are the same
object: (i) \emph{convolution} / the explicit formula (arithmetic); (ii) the reflection
$s\leftrightarrow 1-s$, the functional-equation involution $\iota:\chi\mapsto\zbar$, whose
commutator with the multiplicative-character twist is the family's order parameter
(\S\ref{sec:reflect}); and (iii) Paper~C's algebraic ER$\,=\,$EPR \emph{non-traversability},
$\pi_X([X,Y])=0$ at $|c|<10^{-16}$ --- the bracket opaque from inputs to outputs, which is
``uncrossable for identity'' in geometric dress. The three-way identification is an organising
conjecture with three independently verified instances, not a theorem: it proves nothing about
RH, constructs no Hilbert--P\'olya operator, and Paper~C itself flags the quantitative AdS/CFT
match as open. Reproducible at seed $20260423$; negatives first-class.
\end{abstract}

\section{The correction: seam, not flattening}\label{sec:correct}

\begin{definition}[Flattening and its seam, recalled from \textit{The Reversible Flattening}]
A \emph{flattening} projects a relational object (a factorization, a divisor lattice) onto a
scalar; it is \emph{reversible} iff the projecting operation is a homomorphism into the stored
relational structure. The boundary of reversibility --- the \emph{seam} --- is \emph{convolution}:
the operation that adds indices while multiplying values, ``crossable for density and uncrossable
for identity.''
\end{definition}

The critical line is not a flattening under this definition. It is the seam itself. \textit{The
Geometry Engine} (``Two faces of zeta, T1 both directions'') exhibits the two sides:

\begin{center}\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
 & \textbf{Prime face} & \textbf{Zero face} \\
\midrule
product      & Euler $\prod_p (1-p^{-s})^{-1}$ & Hadamard (over zeros) \\
operation    & multiplicative                  & additive \\
structure    & \emph{unique} (a point/address) & \emph{cardinal} (size of a cloud) \\
recovered    & $\psi(x)$ from zeros ($7.83/7.82,\dots$) & zeros from primes ($+122\sigma$) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The two faces are one object seen through zeta's two products; the explicit formula is the
dictionary, verified both directions on $10^5$ zeros. So the object is two-sided and
two-dimensional (a relational shape, not a scalar), and Re$(s)=\half$ is where the sides are
glued. Calling that a ``flattening to a line'' inverts the picture: nothing was projected down;
the line is the seam of a shape.

\section{Our measurements are the seam law}\label{sec:seamlaw}

The vantage-point census (\texttt{hp\_vantage\_points.py}) found two \emph{function} faces of the
zeros --- arithmetic ($\sim\!830\sigma$) and local-order ($\sim\!26\sigma$) --- over a GUE
silhouette. In seam language these are not two peers: they are the two sides seen from the zero
locus.

\begin{observation}[The two faces are the two sides across the seam]
The arithmetic face is the \emph{prime side imaged across the seam}: the prime-resonance witness
is the Euler product showing through the explicit formula, and its harmonic ladder (rung $k$ at
$k\log p$) carries exactly the convolution weight $p^{-k/2}$ --- verified as a \emph{repetition
tower three deep}: the ratio law holds at slope $-0.501$ (rung~2, $R^2\!=\!1.00$, to $p\le 47$)
and $-1.015$ (rung~3, $R^2\!=\!1.00$, to $p\le 7$ before the $p^{-1}$-suppressed cube line drowns
in background), with an absolute weight fit of slope $+1.002$ and correlation $1.000$ across the
$33$ prime-power lines that clear background (\texttt{hp\_harmonic\_ladder.py},
\texttt{hp\_ladder\_tower.py}). The local-order face is the zero side's own \emph{cardinal}
structure --- the additive density law (GUE), which every marginal-matched spectrum shares.
\end{observation}

And the central number our census produced is the seam law itself. \textit{The Reversible
Flattening} characterises the seam as ``crossable for density, uncrossable for identity.'' Our
data says: arithmetic couples to the \emph{density} (first-order counts $\sim\!0.99$) while
individual spacings stay \emph{generic} (GUE; second-order $\sim\!0.6$). That is the seam law,
re-measured on the zeros without our having named it. The critical line is where multiplication
(shape, density) meets addition (the cloud, identity), and the coupling is exactly one-sided:
density crosses, identity does not.

\section{The reflection is the same seam}\label{sec:reflect}

The functional equation is the symmetry of the completed $\xi(s)$: the reflection
$s\leftrightarrow 1-s$ across Re$(s)=\half$. On the family of Dirichlet $L$-functions it is the
involution $\iota:\chi\mapsto\zbar$. Because $\chi$ is a \emph{multiplicative} character it lives
on the prime face; the twist $T$ paints $\chi(p)^k$ on rung $k$. The rung-1 twist is decisive
and hardens with sample size (complex $\chi$: $R=0.99$ at $\sim\!150$ generated $L$-zeros;
quadratic $\chi$: rung-1 sign flips with $\chi(p)$, $12/12$;
\texttt{hp\_ladder\_character\_twist.py}, \texttt{hp\_twist\_hardened.py}). We keep a first-class
negative: the rung-2 \emph{phase} magnitude does \emph{not} harden --- the $p^2$-line phase sits
at the demodulation noise floor even at $\sim\!150$ $L$-zeros (T3), so the twist \emph{structure}
below is established through the commutator parity law and the resolved rungs, not through the
rung-2 phase resultant.

\begin{principle}[The commutator is the family's order parameter]\label{prin:comm}
With $\|[\iota,T]\|_k=\operatorname*{mean}_p|\operatorname{Im}\chi(p)^k|$, one has
$\|[\iota,T]\|_k=0$ iff $\operatorname{order}(\chi)\mid 2k$; in particular the arithmetic face is
real on every rung iff $\chi$ is self-dual ($\chi\in\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$): \textbf{the self-dual
locus is the commuting locus}, and off it the commutator equals the phase the zeros carry. The
parity law is confirmed to rung~3, including its new statement --- an order-$6$ character commutes
on rung~3 ($6\mid 6$) while an order-$4$ character does not ($4\nmid 6$) --- with mod-$7$ rung~3
phase resolving under $\chi^3$ ($R=0.59$, above floor; \texttt{hp\_twist\_hardened.py}).
\end{principle}

The reflection is not a second structure bolted onto the seam --- it \emph{is} the seam read as
a symmetry. Reality (self-duality) is $[\iota,T]=0$: the value at which reflecting the prime
face and reading it back agree. The apparent puzzle of \S\ref{sec:seamlaw}'s draft ancestor
(real, time-reversal-even arithmetic coefficients coexisting with unitary GUE statistics) is not
a contradiction on a line; it is one side of the seam (the prime face, reflection-even on
$\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$) and the other (the zero face, whose local law is GUE), read together
because they meet there.

\section{The bridge is the same seam}\label{sec:bridge}

Paper~C gives the geometric face. Its \emph{algebraic non-traversability} (Prop., verified in
\texttt{src/paper\_c/er\_epr\_algebraic.py} at $|c|<10^{-16}$) states that for a bracket output
$B=[X,Y]$, the trace projection back to an input vanishes identically, $\pi_X(B)=0$: no
information in $B$ can be traversed back to $X$ by inner-product reconstruction --- the ACS
bracket is ``opaque from inputs to outputs.'' This is the ER$\,=\,$EPR non-traversable bridge
derived from the bracket algebra, no bulk geometry invoked.

\begin{principle}[One property, three dresses]\label{prin:one}
The seam's defining property has three verified statements, conjectured identical:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{arithmetic:} convolution is \emph{crossable for density, uncrossable for
        identity} (\textit{Reversible Flattening}; re-measured $0.99$ vs GUE here);
  \item \textbf{symmetric:} $\iota$ relates the two sides but $[\iota,T]$ does not vanish off
        the self-dual locus (\S\ref{sec:reflect});
  \item \textbf{geometric:} the bracket is \emph{non-traversable}, $\pi_X([X,Y])=0$ (Paper~C).
\end{itemize}
``Density crosses, identity does not'' $=$ ``$\iota$ reflects, $[\iota,T]\neq 0$'' $=$ ``the
bridge is non-traversable.'' One object, three descriptions.
\end{principle}

Non-traversability is also the shape of the Hilbert--P\'olya obstruction, made quantitative
(\texttt{hp\_operator\_constraint.py}). Any self-adjoint $H$ with spectrum $\{\gamma_k\}$ must
carry, simultaneously, \emph{real} periodic-orbit amplitudes ($|\!\operatorname{Im}W|/|\!\operatorname{Re}W|
= 0.0001$, the prime-face reflection-evenness) and \emph{GUE} level repulsion (measured exponent
$\beta = 2.00$, the zero-face local law). The first pulls to a time-reversal-invariant ($\beta=1$)
operator, the second to a time-reversal-broken ($\beta=2$) one.

The companion hypercone test (\texttt{test\_conjecture\_hypercone\_projection.py}, branch
\texttt{claude/torsion-topological-condensation-h6v40k}) supplies the geometry that resolves the
tension: eigenvalue degeneracies of Hermitian families are \emph{cones}, the repulsion exponent
counts the cone's codimension ($\beta = \mathrm{codim} - 1$), and the chirality direction --- the
$i$ the J-map introduces --- is exactly what buys the third dimension (measured on the zeros:
$\beta = 2.019$, a codim-3 shadow). \texttt{hp\_wall\_resolution\_class.py} then establishes,
exactly and by ensemble, that the two wall constraints \emph{coexist} precisely in the class of
families carrying an \emph{anti-commuting} antiunitary symmetry ($C H^{*} C^{-1} = -H$, verified
with $C^2=-1$ on the hypercone's active block): the anti-commuting $C$ pairs the spectrum
$\gamma\leftrightarrow-\gamma$ --- making the full-spectrum witness machine-real --- while
preserving the codim-3 degeneracy ($\beta=2$); a \emph{commuting} antiunitary instead confines the
family to the real slice $z=0$ (codim~2, $\beta=1$). Among GOE / GUE / chiral ensembles, only the
chiral class passes both constraints ($\beta=2.01$, $|\!\operatorname{Im}W|/|W| = 10^{-12.0}$,
pairing defect $10^{-12.2}$). In seam coordinates: \textbf{the amplitudes live on the real slice
(the self-dual locus $\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$); the repulsion counts the hidden imaginary direction
--- the phase direction $[\iota,T]$ measures}. The wall is a class selection, not a contradiction:
$\beta$ is the dimension counter of the projection, and ``the line is a slice of the cone.'' What
remains open --- unchanged --- is the arithmetic realisation: an operator in this class whose orbit
lengths are $\{\log p\}$ with the $p^{-k/2}$ weights. The class is pinned; the primes are not yet
in it.

\section{Why the pieces come in a forced order}\label{sec:assembly}

Because it is one object, the pieces that describe it are a sequence, not a set, and the sequence
is forced: you cannot measure the $\chi^k$ twist before the ladder, cannot place
$\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$ before the twist, cannot isolate the ladder before splitting the faces ---
\[
  \text{faces}\ \prec\ \text{ladder}\ \prec\ \text{twist}\ \prec\ \text{involution}.
\]
No permutation composes into a defined object; the other orders are undefined, not merely
worse.\footnote{The cumulative folk tale is the exact shape: each piece is swallowed \emph{to
catch} the previous one, the order cannot be permuted, the cascade is deterministic, and it has a
terminal state. ``Why is it this way'' is answered by pointing back one dependency edge.} The
bundle MANIFEST lists the pieces as a flat, commutative table; the seam is the same pieces under
the non-commutative composition the table cannot hold. That is the precise sense of ``we had the
pieces, we just assembled them in the wrong sequence.''

\section{Scope and limitations}\label{sec:scope}

Kept first-class.
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{The unification is a conjecture.} \S\ref{sec:bridge}'s three-way identification
        has three \emph{independently verified} instances (seam law, commutator law,
        non-traversability) but no proof that they are one structure. Paper~C explicitly flags the
        quantitative AdS/CFT match as ``a research direction, not a derived consequence''; the
        arithmetic$\leftrightarrow$geometric identification inherits that status.
  \item \textbf{Classical core.} The two-faced explicit-formula reading (Euler/Hadamard,
        convolution seam) is classical (FTA, Hadamard product, Riemann--von Mangoldt). The
        contribution is structural framing and the measured seam constants, not new theorems.
  \item \textbf{Nothing about RH, no operator.} The seam is a spectral fingerprint an operator
        must match; it constructs none and locates no zero.
  \item \textbf{Data-limited rung-2 phase (first-class negative).} Generating $\sim\!150$
        non-circular $L$-zeros hardened the rung-1 twist ($R=0.99$) and confirmed the parity law
        to rung~3, but the rung-2 \emph{phase} magnitude stayed at the demodulation noise floor
        ($\sim\!0.26$) and did \emph{not} improve --- the $p^2$-line phase is below resolution at
        accessible heights (T3). Reported as a negative, not spun. Rung-1 twist, quadratic and
        rung-3 signs, the parity law, and the seam density law are decisive (T1).
  \item \textbf{Tower depth is background-limited.} The $p^{-k/2}$ ladder is resolved to rung~3
        only for small primes ($p\le 7$ at $10^5$ zeros); the cube line drowns beyond, and rung~4
        is not attempted. The repetition tower is confirmed to depth~3 where measurable, no deeper.
\end{itemize}
