\begin{abstract}
Four machine-verified results about the Riemann and Dirichlet zeros, taken together,
say the critical line is not a line but a projection. The witness census
(\texttt{hp\_vantage\_points.py}) finds exactly two \emph{function} faces --- an
arithmetic face and a local-order face --- over a GUE \emph{silhouette} that every
spectrum in the universality class shares. The arithmetic face is a prime periodic-orbit
trace formula: its harmonic ladder (rung $k$ at frequency $k\log p$) obeys the
$p^{-k/2}$ repetition weight of the Weil explicit formula to a log--log slope of
$-0.533$ against $-0.5$ ($R^2=0.98$, \texttt{hp\_harmonic\_ladder.py}). That ladder is
\emph{universal} across $L(s,\chi)$, twisted by $\chi^k$ on rung $k$: for complex
characters the phase-demodulation table is diagonal (rung~1 aligns under $\chi$,
$R=0.99$; rung~2 under $\chi^2$), and for quadratic characters rung~1 flips sign with
$\chi(p)$ while rung~2 is character-blind ($12/12$, \texttt{hp\_ladder\_character\_twist.py}).
The arithmetic face is also \emph{real} (cosine-only) precisely on the self-dual members
(\(\zeta\) and the quadratic $\chi$), and phase-carrying off them. We organise these
facts geometrically. The functional equation is an involution $\chi\mapsto\zbar$ on the
family of $L$-functions; its fixed locus is exactly the self-dual set where the arithmetic
face is real, and its off-locus is exactly where the face carries the $\chi^k$ phase. The
naive puzzle --- real (time-reversal-even) arithmetic coefficients coexisting with unitary
(GUE, time-reversal-broken) local statistics on a single line --- dissolves once the line
is read as one \emph{fiber} of a family fibered over character space: reality is the
fixed-locus coordinate of the involution, GUE is the per-fiber local law, and
``flattening'' is the act of studying one $L$-function in isolation, which superimposes the
two onto a single line. This is an organising framing of verified numerics, not a theorem:
it proves nothing about RH, constructs no Hilbert--P\'olya operator, and the rung-2 twist
is numerically exhibited (T3), not established. Reproducible at seed $20260423$; negatives
first-class.
\end{abstract}

\section{The apparent tension}\label{sec:tension}

Read on a single line, two of these facts pull in opposite directions.

\begin{observation}[The naive puzzle]\label{obs:puzzle}
The arithmetic face of $\zeta$ is \emph{real}: the explicit-formula coefficients
$\Lambda(n)/\sqrt n$ are real, the witness $F(u)=\sum_\gamma\cos(u\gamma)$ is cosine-only,
and the structure is even under $\gamma\mapsto-\gamma$. A real, reflection-even orbit sum is
the fingerprint of a \emph{time-reversal-symmetric} (orthogonal, $\beta=1$) system. Yet the
\emph{local} statistics of the same zeros are GUE --- unitary, $\beta=2$, time-reversal
\emph{broken} (level repulsion $\propto s^2$, not $s$). A single line cannot be both
symmetry classes at once.
\end{observation}

The puzzle is only apparent: the two statements live on different axes (a global reflection
symmetry versus a local level-repulsion exponent), and their coexistence is standard. What
the framework adds is not a resolution of a contradiction --- there is none --- but a
\emph{coordinate system} in which the two facts stop looking like they are about the same
object.

\section{The involution and its fixed locus}\label{sec:involution}

The decisive datum is that ``reality'' is \emph{not universal} across the family. The
$\chi^k$-twist experiment shows the arithmetic face is real only for the self-dual members.

\begin{principle}[Reality is the fixed locus of the functional equation]\label{prin:fix}
The functional equation relates $L(s,\chi)$ to $L(1-s,\zbar)$; on the family of primitive
Dirichlet characters it is the involution $\iota:\chi\mapsto\zbar$. Its fixed locus is the
self-dual set $\{\chi=\zbar\}$: the trivial character ($\zeta$) and the real (quadratic)
characters. The arithmetic face is real \emph{exactly on} $\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$ and carries a
non-trivial phase exactly off it. Concretely, rung $k$ carries $\chi(p)^k$; reality is
$\chi^k\in\mathbb R$ for all $k$, i.e.\ $\chi$ real, i.e.\ $\chi\in\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$.
\end{principle}

This is verified on both sides. On $\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$ (Part~2, quadratic $\chi$): rung~1
flips sign with $\chi(p)=\pm1$ and rung~2 is character-blind because $\chi(p)^2=+1$ ---
$12/12$ across $d=-35,-91,-104$. Off $\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$ (Part~1, complex $\chi$ of order
$4$ and $6$): the face is genuinely complex, and the phase climbs the ladder as $\chi^k$ ---
the demodulation table is diagonal, rung~1 aligning under $\chi$ and rung~2 under $\chi^2$.

Level repulsion is orthogonal to all of this. GUE is the local law on \emph{every} fiber,
self-dual or not (the single-$L$ statistics are unitary in the Katz--Sarnak sense
regardless of the character's order). So the ``$\beta=1$ smell'' of
Observation~\ref{obs:puzzle} was never a local statement: it was the global reflection
symmetry of the \emph{self-dual} fiber, mistaken for a local repulsion class because both
were being read off the same flattened line.

\section{The object, not the line}\label{sec:object}

\begin{principle}[The critical line is one fiber of a fibered object]\label{prin:fiber}
Fix a modulus $q$. The primitive characters mod $q$ index a family of $L$-functions; each
contributes a critical line carrying the two faces of \S\ref{sec:inputs}. The natural object
is the total space of this family, fibered over character space, with
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{ladder coordinate} $k=1,2,3,\dots$ (the repetition rungs, weight $p^{-k/2}$),
  \item \textbf{character coordinate} $\chi$ (the twist $\chi^k$ on rung $k$),
  \item \textbf{involution} $\iota:\chi\mapsto\zbar$ (the functional equation), whose fixed
        locus is the real-arithmetic locus,
  \item \textbf{per-fiber local law} GUE (unitary), independent of the above.
\end{itemize}
A single critical line is the restriction to one $\chi$. ``Flattening'' is that restriction:
it discards the character and ladder coordinates and the involution structure, and projects
the arithmetic face and the local law onto one line, where they superimpose and read as the
tension of Observation~\ref{obs:puzzle}.
\end{principle}

The picture is reversible in the sense the bundle's \emph{Reversible Flattening} thread
intends: the flattened line retains enough to reconstruct the missing coordinates. The
ladder is recovered from the harmonic content of one line (\S\ref{sec:inputs}, rung~2 sits
at $2\log p$ with weight $p^{-1/2}$); the character coordinate is recovered by demodulation
($\chi$ from rung~1, $\chi^2$ from rung~2); and the involution is recovered as the reflection
that fixes the real-arithmetic fibers. What one cannot recover from a single line is the
\emph{fact} that it is one fiber among many --- which is precisely why studying $\zeta$ in
isolation makes the two symmetry aspects look like a contradiction.

\section{Consistency with the ACS transport obstruction}\label{sec:acs}

Paper~B$'$ finds the spectral transport obstruction ``forced non-abelian'' --- its algebraic
character is conjugacy-class-valued, not a central scalar. The fibered picture is consistent
with, and sharpens, that finding: the twist is the character-group action, and the obstruction
to trivialising it across the family is non-abelian exactly when the character group is
non-trivially acted on by $\iota$. The self-dual fixed locus is the abelian (real-scalar)
sublocus; the genuinely complex fibers are where the obstruction acquires its phase. We state
this as consistency, not derivation: no claim here computes the obstruction from the geometry,
and the $\iota$-action is exhibited on three moduli, not proved in general.

\section{Scope and limitations}\label{sec:scope}

Kept first-class, per the bundle's standard.
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Not a theorem.} \S\S\ref{sec:involution}--\ref{sec:object} organise verified
        numerics into a coordinate system. The coexistence of self-duality and GUE is standard
        analytic number theory; the contribution is the mapping (reality $=$ $\mathrm{Fix}(\iota)$;
        twist $=\chi^k$ on rung $k$), not a new result about $L$-functions.
  \item \textbf{Nothing about RH, no operator.} The trace-formula reading of the arithmetic
        face is the Weil explicit formula (known); it constructs no Hilbert--P\'olya operator
        and proves no zero location. The two faces are a spectral fingerprint a candidate
        operator must match, not an operator.
  \item \textbf{Data-limited twist.} The rung-2 phase alignment (Part~1) is directional at
        $\sim\!55$ $L$-zeros: the diagonal beats its off-diagonal, but the absolute
        resultant ($0.21$--$0.34$) is modest and the mod-5 case sits near the random floor.
        Tier T3. The rung-1 twist and the quadratic-sign parity (Part~2) are decisive (T1).
  \item \textbf{One modulus at a time.} The fibered object is exhibited within fixed-$q$
        families; the cross-$q$ organisation (conductor as a further coordinate) is not tested here.
\end{itemize}
